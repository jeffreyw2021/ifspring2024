Hawaiian Loco Moco

A comforting Hawaiian dish featuring white rice topped with a hamburger patty, a fried egg, and rich gravy. Perfect for any meal of the day.

Equipments

  • Rice cooker
  • Fry pan

Dietary Concerns

This recipe uses:
  • Soy Sauce: Contains gluten and soy
  • Beef: Source of animal protein

Ingredients

  • 1 Egg
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 medium sized sweet onion (optional)
  • 3/4 cup sliced mushroom (optional)
  • green onions for garnishing
  • cooked rice
  • Hamburger Patties:
    • 1 1/2 lb ground beef
    • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
    • 1 1/2 tsp seasoning salt
    • 1 tsp garlic powder
    • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Gravy:
    • 1 1/2 cup beef stock
    • 4 tsp shoyu (soy sauce)
    • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
    • 2 tsp ketchup
    • 5 tsp cornstarch

Estimated Cook Time

Rice cook: ~20 min

Make patty: ~10 min

Fry patty: ~20 min

Make gravy: ~5 min

Cook Mushroom & Onion: ~10 min

Fry egg: <3 min

Total: ~48 min

Instructions

  1. Hamburger Patties:

    1. Mix together the ground beef, Worcestershire sauce, seasoning salt, garlic powder, and black pepper in a large bowl.
    2. Start forming it into patties by grabbing a handful of meat and rolling it into a ball. Then press down to flatten it out. Smooth the edges so there isn't any cracks or jagged edges. Patties should come out to at least one inch thick. Try to keep the patties large since it will shrink when fried.
    3. Heat a pan over medium heat. Fry each side of the patties for about 3-5 minutes or until well done.
    4. Set each finished patty on a plate lined with paper towel to catch any extra grease.

  2. Gravy:

    1. Mix together the beef stock, shoyu, Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, and cornstarch. Whisk it until the cornstarch has come off of the bottom.
    2. Using the same pan, melt one tablespoon of the butter to caramelize the onions for about a minute then fry the mushrooms for another minute or two. (It is optional to add the mushrooms and onions. If not, just skip to the next step.)
    3. Melt the rest of the butter and add in the gravy mix to the pan. Keep stirring the gravy until it begins to thicken and simmer.

  3. Egg:

    1. Melt a slice of butter and crack an egg on a frying pan.
    2. Cook a sunny side up egg. (You can switch it up to your preference) Set aside

  4. Assemble Loco Moco:

    1. Scoop a generous serving of hot white rice on a plate. Layer it with one hamburger patty then drizzle on the gravy. Top it with the egg and garnish with green onions.
    2. Serve it hot and enjoy!

Serving

This dish is meant to be enjoyed hot or warm.
